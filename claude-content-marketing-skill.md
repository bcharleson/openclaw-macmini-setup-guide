# Content Marketing - Claude Skill

## Description

Create high-quality, SEO-optimized content for blogs, social media, newsletters, landing pages, and other marketing channels. This skill helps marketing teams produce engaging content that drives traffic, generates leads, and builds brand authority.

## When to Use This Skill

Invoke this skill when users ask about:
- Writing blog posts or articles
- Creating social media content
- SEO optimization and keyword research
- Email newsletter content
- Landing page copy
- Lead magnet content (ebooks, whitepapers, guides)
- Content calendars and strategies
- Repurposing content across channels

## Prerequisites

Before creating content, gather:
- **Target Audience**: Who are you writing for? (personas, roles, industries)
- **Content Goal**: Traffic, leads, education, engagement, brand awareness
- **Topic/Keywords**: What should the content cover?
- **Brand Voice**: Tone, style, personality
- **Distribution Channel**: Blog, LinkedIn, Twitter, email, etc.
- **Competitors**: What content performs well in your space?

## Instructions

### 1. Define Content Objective

Ask the user:
- What's the primary goal? (Awareness, consideration, decision, retention)
- Who is the target audience?
- What action should readers take after consuming?
- What stage of the buyer journey are they in?
- What pain points or questions does this address?

### 2. Research and Ideation

Help with:
- **Keyword Research**: Identify high-volume, low-competition keywords
- **Competitor Analysis**: What's ranking? What gaps exist?
- **Trending Topics**: What's your audience talking about?
- **Customer Questions**: What do support/sales teams hear repeatedly?
- **Content Angles**: Fresh perspectives on common topics

### 3. Choose Content Format

Determine the best format:
- **Blog Posts**: Long-form thought leadership (1,500-3,000 words)
- **How-To Guides**: Step-by-step tutorials (1,000-2,000 words)
- **Listicles**: "X Ways to..." (800-1,500 words)
- **Case Studies**: Customer success stories (1,000-1,500 words)
- **Comparison Posts**: "A vs B" or "Best tools for X" (1,500-2,500 words)
- **Social Posts**: Platform-specific (varies by channel)
- **Email Newsletters**: Curated insights (300-600 words)

### 4. Create Content Outline

Structure content with:
- **Hook**: Compelling opening that captures attention
- **Introduction**: Set context and promise value
- **Body**: Main content with clear sections/subheadings
- **Examples**: Real-world applications, data, stories
- **Visuals**: Where images, charts, or diagrams would help
- **Conclusion**: Summarize key takeaways
- **CTA**: Clear next step for readers

### 5. Write SEO-Optimized Content

Apply SEO best practices:
- **Title Tag**: Include primary keyword, under 60 characters
- **Meta Description**: Compelling summary, 150-160 characters
- **Headings**: H1 (title), H2 (main sections), H3 (subsections)
- **Keyword Placement**: Naturally in title, intro, headings, conclusion
- **Internal Links**: Link to related content on your site
- **External Links**: Link to authoritative sources
- **Image Alt Text**: Describe images with relevant keywords
- **URL Structure**: Short, descriptive, keyword-rich

### 6. Optimize for Readability

Ensure content is scannable:
- **Short paragraphs**: 2-4 sentences max
- **Bullet points**: Break up text, highlight key points
- **Subheadings**: Every 200-300 words
- **Bold/italics**: Emphasize important concepts
- **White space**: Don't overwhelm with text walls
- **Visuals**: Break up content with images, charts, quotes
- **Readability score**: Aim for 8th grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid)

### 7. Add Persuasive Elements

Incorporate:
- **Data and statistics**: Credibility and authority
- **Customer quotes**: Social proof
- **Expert opinions**: Industry authority
- **Personal stories**: Relatability and connection
- **Actionable tips**: Immediate value
- **CTAs**: Guide readers to next step

### 8. Create Platform-Specific Variations

Repurpose content for:

**LinkedIn Post:**
- Professional tone
- Hook in first 2 lines (above "see more")
- 1,300 characters max for engagement
- Include carousel or document for depth
- Ask a question to drive comments

**Twitter/X Thread:**
- Start with hook tweet
- One idea per tweet
- 8-12 tweets optimal
- Use line breaks for readability
- End with CTA and link

**Email Newsletter:**
- Compelling subject line (< 50 chars)
- Personalized opening
- Scannable sections
- Single clear CTA
- P.S. for urgency or bonus value

**Instagram Caption:**
- Hook in first line
- Story-driven
- Emojis for visual breaks
- Hashtags (5-10 relevant ones)
- CTA in caption and comments

### 9. Optimize for Conversion

Include strategic CTAs:
- **Top of funnel**: "Download guide", "Subscribe to newsletter"
- **Middle of funnel**: "Watch demo", "See case studies"
- **Bottom of funnel**: "Start free trial", "Book a call"
- **Placement**: Throughout content (intro, mid-article, conclusion)
- **Design**: Use buttons, banners, or inline links

### 10. Create Content Calendar

Plan content strategically:
- **Weekly blog posts**: Consistent publishing schedule
- **Social media cadence**: 3-5x per week on key channels
- **Email newsletters**: Weekly or bi-weekly
- **Seasonal content**: Plan around industry events, holidays
- **Evergreen vs timely**: Balance lasting value with trending topics

## Output Format

Provide:
1. **Title Options**: 3-5 headline variations
2. **Meta Description**: SEO-optimized summary
3. **Full Content**: Complete post/article with formatting
4. **Key Takeaways**: Bullet-point summary
5. **Repurposing Ideas**: How to use across other channels
6. **Suggested Images**: What visuals would enhance content
7. **Related Topics**: Ideas for follow-up content

## Content Templates

### Blog Post Template

```markdown
# [Compelling Title with Primary Keyword]

[Hook: Surprising stat, provocative question, or relatable pain point]

[Introduction: Set context, promise value, preview what's to come]

## [H2: Main Section 1]

[Content with examples, data, and actionable tips]

### [H3: Subsection if needed]

[Detailed explanation or step-by-step guidance]

## [H2: Main Section 2]

[Continue pattern]

## Key Takeaways

- [Bullet point 1]
- [Bullet point 2]
- [Bullet point 3]

## Conclusion

[Summarize value delivered, reinforce main point]

[Strong CTA: What should reader do next?]
```

### LinkedIn Post Template

```
[Hook: Bold statement or question]

[Context: Why this matters]

[Core insight or framework]

[Personal story or example]

[Actionable takeaway]

[Question to drive engagement]

[Optional: CTA with link in comments]
```

### Email Newsletter Template

```
Subject: [Curiosity-driven or value-focused]

Hi [Name],

[Personal opening - conversational tone]

[This week's insights:]

1. [Insight 1 with brief explanation]
2. [Insight 2 with brief explanation]
3. [Insight 3 with brief explanation]

[Featured content: Link to full blog post or resource]

[Clear CTA button]

[Sign off]

P.S. [Bonus tip, urgency, or human touch]
```

## SEO Best Practices

### Keyword Research

Use tools to find:
- **Primary keyword**: Main topic (high volume, moderate competition)
- **Secondary keywords**: Related terms (long-tail variations)
- **LSI keywords**: Semantically related terms
- **Question keywords**: What people actually ask

**Tools**: Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Keyword Planner, AnswerThePublic

### On-Page SEO Checklist

- [ ] Primary keyword in title
- [ ] Primary keyword in URL
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Secondary keywords in H2 headings
- [ ] Meta description optimized
- [ ] Image alt text includes keywords
- [ ] Internal links to related content (3-5)
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (2-3)
- [ ] Mobile-friendly formatting
- [ ] Page load speed optimized

### Content Optimization

- **Word count**: 1,500-3,000 words for competitive keywords
- **Readability**: Flesch Reading Ease score 60-70
- **Keyword density**: 1-2% (natural usage, no stuffing)
- **Multimedia**: Include images, videos, infographics
- **Schema markup**: Article, FAQ, How-To structured data

## Social Media Best Practices

### LinkedIn

- **Optimal length**: 1,000-1,300 characters
- **Posting time**: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant ones
- **Engagement**: Respond to comments within first hour
- **Format**: Native posts outperform link posts

### Twitter/X

- **Optimal length**: 240-280 characters (allows for RTs with comments)
- **Posting time**: Weekdays, 8-10 AM and 6-9 PM
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 max
- **Threads**: 8-12 tweets for depth
- **Visuals**: Include image or video for 2x engagement

### Instagram

- **Optimal length**: 138-150 characters
- **Posting time**: Weekdays, 11 AM - 1 PM
- **Hashtags**: 5-10 in caption or first comment
- **Carousel posts**: 10 slides, tell a story
- **Reels**: 15-30 seconds, trending audio

## Content Ideation

### Types of Content That Perform

1. **How-To Guides**: Step-by-step instructions
2. **Listicles**: "10 Ways to...", "5 Best..."
3. **Case Studies**: Customer success stories
4. **Data Studies**: Original research and insights
5. **Expert Roundups**: Multiple perspectives on one topic
6. **Comparisons**: "A vs B", "Best tools for X"
7. **Thought Leadership**: Hot takes, predictions, frameworks
8. **Behind-the-Scenes**: Company culture, process deep-dives

### Content Formula (AIDA)

- **Attention**: Grab with compelling hook
- **Interest**: Build with relevant insights
- **Desire**: Show transformation possible
- **Action**: Clear CTA for next step

## Metrics to Track

Help users measure content performance:
- **Organic traffic**: Sessions from search engines
- **Engagement**: Time on page, scroll depth, bounce rate
- **Social shares**: LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook shares
- **Backlinks**: Other sites linking to your content
- **Conversions**: Newsletter signups, demo requests, downloads
- **Keyword rankings**: Position for target keywords
- **Click-through rate**: From search results to your page

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Writing for search engines, not humans** - Keyword stuffing
❌ **No clear CTA** - What should readers do next?
❌ **Poor formatting** - Long paragraphs, no visuals
❌ **Not repurposing** - Create once, distribute everywhere
❌ **Ignoring analytics** - Double down on what works
❌ **Inconsistent publishing** - Set a schedule and stick to it
❌ **Generic content** - Add unique insights, data, or stories

## Advanced Techniques

### Content Clustering

Create topic clusters:
- **Pillar page**: Comprehensive guide on broad topic (3,000+ words)
- **Cluster content**: 8-12 supporting articles on subtopics
- **Internal linking**: All cluster content links to pillar page
- **SEO benefit**: Establish topical authority

### Skyscraper Technique

Improve on existing content:
1. Find top-ranking content for your keyword
2. Analyze what makes it successful
3. Create something significantly better (more comprehensive, updated, better design)
4. Reach out to sites linking to original, share your improved version

### Content Refresh Strategy

Update existing content:
- Add new data and examples
- Improve SEO optimization
- Enhance visuals and formatting
- Update outdated information
- Republish with new date
