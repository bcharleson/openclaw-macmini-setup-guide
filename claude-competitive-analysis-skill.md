# Competitive Analysis - Claude Skill

## Description

Conduct comprehensive competitive intelligence research to understand your competitive landscape, identify market opportunities, and develop winning positioning strategies. This skill helps teams gather, analyze, and act on competitive insights.

## When to Use This Skill

Invoke this skill when users ask about:
- Analyzing competitors and their strategies
- Competitive positioning and differentiation
- Market landscape mapping
- Win/loss analysis
- Pricing and packaging benchmarking
- Competitive battlecards for sales
- SWOT analysis
- Market opportunity identification

## Prerequisites

Before starting competitive analysis, gather:
- **Your Positioning**: How you describe your product/solution
- **Known Competitors**: Direct and indirect competitors
- **Market Category**: What market/category are you competing in?
- **Target Customers**: Who are you competing for?
- **Strategic Goals**: What decisions will this analysis inform?

## Instructions

### 1. Identify Competitors

Map your competitive landscape:

**Direct Competitors:**
- Solve the same problem
- Target the same customers
- Similar product approach

**Indirect Competitors:**
- Solve the same problem differently
- Alternative approaches

**Potential Competitors:**
- Adjacent players that could enter your space
- Well-funded startups

**Substitutes:**
- Non-obvious alternatives
- "Do nothing" or manual processes
- In-house solutions

**Ask the user:**
- Who do you lose deals to most often?
- Who do prospects mention during sales calls?
- Who appears in comparison searches?
- Who targets your same ICP?

### 2. Gather Competitive Intelligence

**Public Sources:**
- Company website (especially /about, /pricing, /customers)
- Product pages and feature lists
- Blog and content strategy
- Press releases and news coverage
- Social media (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Job postings (what they're hiring for)
- Crunchbase/PitchBook (funding, investors)
- Patent filings

**Review Sites:**
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Gartner Peer Insights
- Read both positive and negative reviews
- Note common complaints and praised features
- Check review dates for trends

**Customer Insights:**
- Win/loss interviews with prospects
- Churn interviews (why customers left for competitors)
- Sales call recordings
- Customer advisory board feedback

**Product Research:**
- Sign up for free trials
- Watch product demos and webinars
- Request sales demos (ethically, not under false pretenses)
- Study documentation and help centers
- Test features and user experience

**SEO & Content:**
- What keywords are they ranking for?
- What content topics are they covering?
- Backlink profile and domain authority
- Paid search strategy (ads they're running)

**Financial & Business:**
- Funding rounds and investors
- Revenue estimates (via tools like Owler)
- Employee count and growth rate
- Office locations and expansion
- M&A activity

### 3. Analyze Core Positioning

For each competitor, document:

**Positioning Statement:**
- How do they describe what they do?
- What value proposition do they lead with?
- What's their tagline/main message?

**Target Audience:**
- What customer segments do they focus on?
- Company size, industry, role
- Use cases they emphasize

**Key Messaging:**
- Top 3 benefits they highlight
- Proof points and differentiators
- Brand voice and tone

**Category Definition:**
- What category do they claim?
- How do they define the problem?
- How do they frame the solution?

### 4. Feature & Capability Comparison

Create a feature matrix:

**Core Features:**
- List all key features in your category
- Rate each competitor: Full support, Partial, None, Unknown
- Note unique features they have that you don't

**Categories to analyze:**
- Core functionality
- Integrations
- Security and compliance
- Scalability and performance
- User experience
- Mobile/platform support
- API and extensibility
- Reporting and analytics

**For each feature:**
- Do they have it?
- How mature is it?
- How easy is it to use?
- Do customers mention it in reviews?

### 5. Pricing & Packaging Analysis

Benchmark pricing strategies:

**Pricing Model:**
- Per user, per feature, usage-based, flat fee
- Monthly vs annual
- Freemium, free trial, or paid-only

**Pricing Tiers:**
- How many tiers?
- What's included in each?
- What's the entry price?
- What's the high-end price?

**Packaging Strategy:**
- How are features bundled?
- What's gated behind higher tiers?
- Add-ons and upsells

**Discounting:**
- Annual discount %
- Volume discounts
- Non-profit/education pricing
- Transparent vs. enterprise quotes

**Total Cost of Ownership:**
- Implementation costs
- Training requirements
- Ongoing support costs

### 6. Go-to-Market Strategy

Analyze their GTM approach:

**Sales Model:**
- Self-service vs. sales-led
- Inside sales vs. field sales
- Partner/channel strategy
- Sales cycle length (from reviews)

**Marketing Strategy:**
- Primary channels (paid, organic, events, etc.)
- Content marketing focus
- Community building
- Brand awareness campaigns
- Thought leadership positioning

**Customer Acquisition:**
- Top traffic sources
- Conversion funnel approach
- Lead magnets and free resources
- Demo/trial strategy

**Customer Success:**
- Onboarding approach
- Support model (24/7, business hours, tiers)
- Community and resources
- Account management for enterprise

### 7. Competitive Strengths & Weaknesses

**SWOT Analysis for each competitor:**

**Strengths:**
- What are they really good at?
- What do customers love?
- Competitive advantages

**Weaknesses:**
- What do they struggle with?
- Common complaints in reviews
- Feature gaps

**Opportunities:**
- What could they do better?
- Market trends in their favor
- Potential strategic moves

**Threats:**
- What could disrupt them?
- Competitive pressure
- Market shifts

### 8. Win/Loss Analysis

Understand why deals are won or lost:

**Why You Win:**
- What made you the better choice?
- Key differentiators that mattered
- Specific features or capabilities
- Pricing/value perception
- Sales process excellence
- Trust and brand factors

**Why You Lose:**
- What made them choose the competitor?
- Feature gaps that mattered
- Pricing concerns
- Brand/trust factors
- Sales process issues
- Timing or urgency factors

**Process:**
- Interview recent wins and losses (within 30 days)
- Ask open-ended questions
- Look for patterns across 20+ interviews
- Quantify reasons (% of deals where X was a factor)

### 9. Create Competitive Battlecards

Develop sales enablement resources:

**Competitor Overview:**
- One-line description
- Target customers
- Key strengths
- Key weaknesses

**Positioning:**
- How they position against you
- How you should position against them
- Key differentiation points

**Objection Handling:**
- Common objections you'll hear
- How to respond effectively
- Proof points to share

**Questions to Ask:**
- Discovery questions that expose their weaknesses
- Questions that highlight your strengths

**Landmines:**
- Topics to avoid
- Where they have the advantage
- How to navigate those conversations

**Key Messages:**
- 3-5 talking points
- Supporting evidence
- Customer stories/proof

**Deal Signals:**
- When to compete aggressively
- When to walk away
- Red flags for bad fit

### 10. Strategic Recommendations

Synthesize insights into action:

**Product:**
- Feature gaps to address
- Opportunities to differentiate
- Areas to double down on

**Pricing:**
- Pricing adjustments
- Packaging changes
- Value proposition enhancement

**Marketing:**
- Positioning refinement
- Messaging updates
- Content opportunities
- Channel priorities

**Sales:**
- Competitive talk tracks
- Discovery question updates
- Demo flow improvements
- Objection handling

**Strategy:**
- Market opportunities
- Partnership possibilities
- Acquisition targets
- Competitive threats to monitor

## Output Format

Provide:
1. **Competitive Landscape Map**: Visual of all competitors by category/positioning
2. **Feature Comparison Matrix**: Spreadsheet of features across competitors
3. **Competitor Profiles**: Deep-dive on top 3-5 competitors
4. **Battlecards**: Sales-ready competitive positioning (1-2 pages per competitor)
5. **Win/Loss Summary**: Key findings from deal analysis
6. **Strategic Recommendations**: Top 5 actions to take
7. **Monitoring Plan**: What to track and how often

## Competitive Intelligence Framework

### The 4 Ps of Competitive Analysis

**Product:**
- Features and capabilities
- User experience
- Technical architecture
- Roadmap (from job posts, customer requests)

**Pricing:**
- Pricing model and tiers
- Entry price and high-end price
- Discounting strategies
- ROI/value claims

**Positioning:**
- Target audience
- Value proposition
- Category definition
- Key messages

**Promotion:**
- Marketing channels
- Content strategy
- Sales approach
- Partnership strategy

### Competitive Positioning Models

**2x2 Matrix:**
- X-axis: [Key differentiator 1]
- Y-axis: [Key differentiator 2]
- Plot yourself and all competitors
- Identify white space opportunities

**Perceptual Map:**
- Map how customers perceive competitors
- Based on survey data or review analysis
- Attributes that matter most to buyers

**Strategic Group Analysis:**
- Group competitors by strategy
- Price/performance segment
- Market focus (SMB vs Enterprise)
- Product approach (all-in-one vs best-of-breed)

## Analysis Techniques

### Review Mining

Extract insights from customer reviews:
1. **Export reviews** from G2, Capterra, TrustRadius
2. **Tag by theme**: Features, Support, Ease of Use, Value, etc.
3. **Sentiment analysis**: Positive vs negative mentions
4. **Trend analysis**: Are complaints increasing or decreasing?
5. **Comparison extraction**: When do they mention your product?

**Key metrics:**
- Star rating trend (going up or down?)
- Response rate to reviews
- Review recency (are they recent?)
- Reviewer profiles (do they match your ICP?)

### Traffic & SEO Analysis

Understand their digital presence:
- **Organic traffic**: Use SimilarWeb, SEMrush, Ahrefs
- **Top pages**: What content drives traffic?
- **Top keywords**: What are they ranking for?
- **Backlink profile**: Who's linking to them?
- **Paid keywords**: What are they bidding on?
- **Traffic sources**: Direct, organic, paid, social, referral

### Job Posting Analysis

Learn their priorities from hiring:
- What roles are they hiring for?
- What skills are they seeking?
- What projects/initiatives are mentioned?
- New locations = expansion plans
- Engineering roles = product roadmap hints
- Sales roles = market expansion

### Social Listening

Monitor what people say:
- Twitter/X mentions and sentiment
- LinkedIn posts and engagement
- Reddit discussions
- Quora questions
- Community forums

**Tools**: Brand24, Mention, Social Searcher

## Metrics to Track

Help users monitor competitors over time:

**Product:**
- New features launched per quarter
- Product releases frequency
- Feature parity score (% of features you both have)

**Marketing:**
- Website traffic (monthly trend)
- Content publishing frequency
- Social media followers and engagement
- Email list growth (estimated)

**Business:**
- Employee count (LinkedIn)
- Funding rounds
- Customer count (if public)
- Press mentions

**Pricing:**
- Price changes
- New tiers or packaging
- Promotional offers

**Perception:**
- G2 rating trend
- Review volume and sentiment
- Brand search volume

**Create a Dashboard:**
- Update monthly or quarterly
- Track 10-15 key metrics
- Alert on significant changes

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Obsessing over competitors** - Focus on customers first
❌ **Feature-for-feature copying** - Differentiate, don't clone
❌ **Ignoring indirect competitors** - They can steal customers too
❌ **Outdated intelligence** - Competitors evolve, update regularly
❌ **No clear action** - Analysis without action is wasted effort
❌ **Only looking at direct competitors** - Consider adjacent markets
❌ **Assuming competitor's strategy** - Validate with real data

## Advanced Techniques

### Competitive Wargaming

Simulate competitor moves:
1. **Assemble teams**: Each team represents a competitor
2. **Define scenario**: Market change, new entrant, etc.
3. **Strategize**: What would each competitor do?
4. **Counter-strategize**: How would you respond?
5. **Learn**: Identify blind spots and opportunities

### Market Share Estimation

Estimate relative market position:
- **Bottom-up**: Customer counts, employee data, reviews
- **Top-up**: TAM/SAM estimates, market reports
- **Proxy metrics**: Web traffic, app downloads, social following

### Churn Analysis

Understand why customers leave for competitors:
- Exit interviews with churned customers
- What did the competitor offer that you didn't?
- Was it product, price, or service?
- How long did they use you before switching?
- Can you win them back?
